Pipeline overview

The pipeline below presents our current discovery-stage target classes. Each track is advanced via high-throughput screening (HTS), hit confirmation, and a defined optimisation path toward translational readouts and program selection.

Illustrative representation of development stages. Program naming, indication selection, and staging will be refined as data emerge.

Discovery tracks

These target classes reflect mitochondrial and metabolic control points that may create leverage in difficult-to-treat disease biology. Each track is milestone-gated and designed to generate decision-quality data early.

Bcl-2 inhibition

Modulate apoptotic priming and survival dependency in relevant tumour contexts.

MCT-1 inhibition

Disrupt lactate export and metabolic coupling in glycolysis-dominant settings.

Lactate dehydrogenase inhibition

Reduce glycolytic throughput where lactate production sustains tumour fitness.

Pyruvate dehydrogenase kinase inhibition

Shift flux toward oxidative metabolism and re-balance mitochondrial function.

AMPK activation / mTOR inhibition

Recalibrate energy sensing and growth signalling with biomarker-guided dosing logic.

Acidosis modulation

Target pH-linked invasion and immune suppression within the tumour microenvironment.

Immune activation

Support immune engagement in settings where metabolism constrains immune function.

Antimicrobial

Where relevant, address microbial contributions to inflammatory and oncogenic signalling.

Mitochondrial repair

Restore bioenergetic integrity and signalling fidelity in stressed cellular environments.

External asset strategy

In parallel with internal discovery, we will evaluate opportunities to acquire or in-license assets that were previously developed and later discontinued by industry. This can accelerate timelines when prior chemistry, safety, or translational learnings exist — provided the asset fits our mitochondria-centric hypothesis and can be advanced with a clear protocolised plan.

What we look for

Mechanistic alignment, credible IP position, and actionable development path.

What we verify

Reproducible biology, clean confirmation data, and practical PK/PD readouts.

Why it matters

De-risk discovery while preserving optionality for fast-moving programs.

Seed-stage milestones (template)

  • Assay build & validation: functional readouts with reproducibility and counterscreens
  • HTS → hit confirmation: orthogonal validation and early mechanism-of-action evidence
  • Hit → lead criteria: potency, selectivity, cellular activity, and a translational biomarker plan
  • Program selection: prioritise 1–2 lead programs with decision-quality data
