Science · mitochondria-centric oncology

High-throughput discovery for under-explored mitochondrial targets.

We combine a mitochondria-centric view of cancer biology with high-throughput screening to identify small-molecule candidates for molecular targets that are often technically challenging or under-prioritised. Programs are advanced using a protocolised, measurement-first development approach.

Platform thesis

Cancer cells frequently adapt their metabolism to survive stress, evade immune pressure, and persist under therapy. Because mitochondria sit at the centre of energy production, redox balance, apoptosis signalling, and biosynthetic flux, mitochondrial control points can act as high-leverage determinants of tumour behaviour and treatment response.

Our scientific focus is not “one target, one drug, one disease.” Instead, we build programs around measurable mitochondrial and metabolic states — then develop small-molecule approaches to modulate them with clarity and discipline.

Mitochondrial control points

Bioenergetics, redox, apoptotic priming, and stress signalling as program-defining levers.

Small-molecule first

Preference for tractable, optimisable chemistry with intracellular access and measurable PK/PD paths.

Measurement-first execution

Assays and biomarkers built early, so programs advance on data rather than narrative.

High-throughput screening and hit discovery

We use high-throughput screening to identify small-molecule starting points against mitochondrial and metabolic targets that can be overlooked for strategic, technical, or historical reasons. Our goal is to turn strong mechanistic logic into actionable chemistry by building assays that report on function — not just binding.

Assay strategy

Prioritise functional readouts (e.g., mitochondrial state, redox stress, cell-fate markers) and confirm in orthogonal assays to reduce artefacts early.

Hit confirmation

Re-test, counterscreen, and validate mechanism-of-action signals before investing in chemistry optimisation.

Optimisation path

Move from hit → lead with defined criteria: potency, selectivity, cellular activity, and a credible translational plan.

Protocolised program development

A mitochondria-centric hypothesis is only useful if it translates into a program that can be executed. We therefore design programs as protocolised development packages: defined patient logic, measurable biomarkers, and milestone-driven experiments that connect mechanism to clinical signal.

Program definition

Indication selection + mechanistic rationale + clear go/no-go criteria.

Translational readouts

Biomarkers and functional endpoints aligned to the mitochondrial/metabolic state the program targets.

Clinical discipline

Protocol design that prioritises measurement, patient stratification hypotheses, and early signal detection.

Mechanistic targeting framework

The platform is designed to apply coordinated pressure across survival signalling, metabolic coupling, and immune context. Example target classes include:

  • Bcl-2 modulation — influence apoptotic priming and survival dependency
  • MCT-1 modulation — disrupt lactate export and metabolic coupling
  • LDH modulation — reduce glycolytic dominance where relevant
  • PDK modulation — shift flux toward oxidative metabolism
  • AMPK / mTOR axis — recalibrate energy sensing and growth signalling
  • Acidosis / pH biology — reduce acid-mediated invasion and immune suppression
  • Immune engagement — design programs compatible with effective immune activity
  • Antimicrobial context (where relevant) — address microbial contributions to inflammatory signalling
  • Mitochondrial repair — restore bioenergetic integrity and signalling fidelity

We communicate our science responsibly: programs are hypothesis-driven and milestone-gated, with claims anchored to measurable validation.
